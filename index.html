Everything you need for storytime success!

A library storytime is a program where a librarian or reads books to a group of young children, usually between the ages of 0 and 5 years old. The goal is to encourage a love for reading and to develop early literacy skills, such as listening comprehension and vocabulary building. Storytime may also include songs, rhymes, and other interactive activities to engage the children and make the experience fun and educational.
The elements of a successful library storytime typically include engaging books, interactive storytelling, songs, rhymes, fingerplays, and movement activities that are developmentally appropriate for the age group of the audience. It's also important to create a welcoming and inclusive environment that encourages participation and promotes a love of reading.
The Every Child Ready to Read program is an early literacy initiative focused on providing parents and caregivers with tools and techniques to help young children develop pre-reading skills. The program emphasizes the importance of talking, singing, reading, writing, and playing as essential activities to promote literacy development in children. It is a collaborative effort between public libraries and other community organizations to support early childhood education.
